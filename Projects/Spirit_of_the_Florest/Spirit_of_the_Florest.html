Project Devlog

Project Crawler is a 3rd person rogue-like dungeon crawler game, where your health is also your currency and ability resource. creating a risk vs reward gameplay. Equip yourself with new abilities and upgrade them at the cost of your health. Project Crawler was created for the university of Portsmouth game jam with a team of 6 members, with the game’s topic being ‘a dungeon crawler with a single resource’.

Key Contributions
Character Controller


I developed the character controller system to allow for freedom of movement and give as much control to the player allowing for quick and precise movement to avoid incoming attacks

Combat System

I developed the player to have two states for both melee (or walking) and ranged, as each need their own player rotation system and animation controller. I created these systems to be fast and fluid to avoid interrupting the flow of the game.


Ability System


I create a large set of systems for unlocking, equipping, using abilities and handling upgrades. All these systems work together to make the flow of acquiring and using abilities as streamlined as possible, to not slow down the pace of gameplay.

modual


I designed a modular ability system that contains the majority of an ability’s logic (Cooldowns, Cost, Casting, Charge handler, Checks, ect) and can be inherited by an ability script, allowing for simple implementation of abilities and project wide alterations to the logic system.

Introduction

For the beginning of the new project, we were tasked with crating a game from a of list options:

• Starship bridge simulator
• University tycoon
• Dungeon crawler with one resource
• A game suitable for someone visually impaired
• 2d platformer within a 3d world
• Lockdown – the game
• A game using an unusual control device

Once the team had formed, we researched the options and discussed potential gameplay loops, ideas and mechanicals for each option. This decision narrowed down list and the team seemed to be mainly interested in the dungeon crawler idea, including myself. This discussed formed the main gameplay loop of having the player with non-regenerating health and no health cap, but by killing enemies they would drop health that can be picked up. The health could be spent on potential abilities, items and upgrades as each level would increase the difficulty as well as adding a sense of risk and reward to the players choices.

There were still some key decisions that the team had not decided on yet such as the player perspective, either being top down, isometrics or third person.

The team easily decided to use unity for this project as we used it for the previous project and the team was already familiar using the software.

I was assigned as a lead programmer for the project with 1 other programmer. I was eager to start on the project but with designs and ideas still undecided, I begin to envision general mechanicals and develop a very primitive version of them.

11th Dec 2020 - 18th Dec 2020
Basic systems and proof of concepts
Primitive Skill Tree

I began developing a skill tree system using unity’s UI system, this system would be the most likely way of obtaining new abilities in the game. I constructed a skill tree node script so that in order to unlock a node, there will need to be enough connected parent nodes unlocked, later development on the system will requiring the player to have enough health to unlock them, once the health system is setup. I also made the UI element go red, yellow or green depending on their status as well as adding a UI text element displaying how many parent nodes are required to be unlocked.

Primitive Inventory System

This inventory system if very basic and can only hold objects, move them or swap them with another object, with the development of this system only taking less than an hour. Though the current system is extremely basic, it is to ensure that if the system is never used, that not too much time would have been wasted on its development, as well as the system could need to be overhaled depending on what the project calls for, preventing potential rewriting of the code. The system uses item as gameobjects within slot gameobjects. When clicking and holding, it stores the objects reference within the curser and when releasing, runs a bunch of checks to determine what to do, such as detecting if the curser is over a slot or not, then if it is, check if the slot is empty, if not, swapping slots with each item.

Basic Isometric Concept

This isometric concept was developed mostly out of curiosity and not directly for the project, though once I added a sword to the character and some interactable objects, it allowed for a better understanding of how an isometric version of the project would look and feel, as currently the player perspective has not yet been decided, and may potentially be used if the project uses an isometric view.

11th Dec 2020 – 18th Jan 2021
Time off, Ideas and Design

This large portion of inactive development on the project was due to the Christmas break as well as the team as a whole deciding the type of gameplay and aesthetics the project should take on. During this time, I personally worked on other projects in the meantime, but unfortunately as eager as I was to work on this project, I was unable to for this duration of time as the project have not cemented any gameplay ideas or fully developed mechanics, and working on other potential mechanics would be a risky use of time as it is possible for ideas not be used in the project at all.

The artsiest also wanted to start designing the characters and the world. off the bat the team knew that we wanted a polygonal art style, as it is both an appealing art style as well as being a non-time-consuming style to develop. ideas then flew around a sprit character either humanoid or a creature, originally starts with a wolf or bear character that may potently wield weapons with its mouth, but there was large hesitancy as it would have been difficult to make a creature wield a weapon in a believable manor. Then ideas turned to making the character a spirt humanoid with an arm for weapons, poetically changing the arm for different weapons. Inspiration for this idea mainly comes from the main character of the prototype games, as they have a very slimier design for a weaponised arm.

By the end of the two-mouth long design discussion with the team, the gameplay will be a 3rd person dungeon crawler with potentially character upgrades and/or an inventory and equipment system, depending on how well the project progresses and to avoid making the scope of the game too large to complete. The art style of the game will be a dark and polygonal theme, with lights illumination the levels, directing the player on where to go.

Implementation was simple, I had setup a prefab and that would be instantiated, passing the damage values and crit to it.

16th Apr 2021 - 22th Apr 2021
Sword Slam & Double jump Abilities, Ability upgrades & Pause menu
Sword slam ability

The sword slam ability is likely going to be the most complex ability in the game, and thus, took a decent time to develop. This ability uses the charges system that’s a part of the modular ability script, so developing the charges aspect of this ability is mostly already done.

The design of the ability is that the player gains charges on successful melee attacks, when the player has three or more charges, they can cast the ability, preforming a slam on the ground, dealing large amounts of damage and knockback. The downsides of using this ability are that the final attack on the melee attack combo is no longer possible for the player to preform, meaning they can no longer utilise its large knockback effect on its own.

This design creates a small trade off and may not always be useful in every situation, such as bosses.

Development of this ability did not cost too much time to make. As the it reuses the animation, hit box and attack functions already crated for the project.

Double jump ability

the double jump ability is likely the quickest and simplest implementation of a feature in the project, as it simply checks if the player is grounded, if not they it will call the jump function in the player controller, while also triggering a bool the prevents the player for jumping multiple time in the air, as this would be un balanced and could be used to find exploits.

Ability upgrades

the ability upgrade system uses the same node structure script from the skill tree, with a few alterations to function better as upgrades instead.

This system still uses the parent unlock and cost requirement for the player to unlock it, but instead, when the player dose unlock the upgrade, it sets level value in a global script that stores the abilities current level. This refreshes the abilities to apply the newly unlocked upgrade, as well as whenever the player equips an ability, it checks the global values to check the current levels and applies them.

There are three different types; cost, cooldown and charges. The cost and cooldown simply lower the cost and max cooldown of the ability, but the charges upgrade (currently only available for the double jump) adds cast changes which allows the player to cast the ability again if they have any remaining charges, else they will regenerate at the ability cooldown rate.

Pause menu

A pause menu is a fairly important element for a game to have, allowing players to take a quick break as well as giving players options to alter any default aspect of the game.

Mouse sensitivity for a 3rd person game is important because it is for easy for players to feel unconfinable if a mouse sensitivity is too high or too low.

I used unity’s player pref to store the players settings data as it is a simple mentored to store non-important data.

Burst and Dash ability, Weapon icon switch animation

This week I aimed to include two abilities I been planning to add, the burst fire and dash ability. I wanted to keep the abilities I add simple to cut down on development time as the project was starting to reach its deadlines, and I wanted to ensure that all the ideas and mechanics that I feel are important in including are finished before the project’s deadline. Burst Ability

The burst ability was a simple inclusion was it mainly consisted of using the main ranged fire function, but instead repeating it 3 times, the one issue I had when developing this ability was that the player could un aim while the burst fire is still going, allowing the player to melee as well as the bolt appearing Infront of the player. The solution I had for this was to create an aim lock, that whenever the ability is still firing, the player will be unable to stop aiming. The player will stop aiming as soon the ability has stopped casting to avoid making the player feel clunky or unresponsive

Dash Ability

Once again, the dash ability was another easy ability implementation, as it simply used the unity character controller and have it move forward for a certain amount of time, also reusing one of the melee attack animations that fit well for this action. It also uses the melee attack hitbox to damage enemies the player collides with. With the reuse of pre-existing asserts, it made the development of this ability fairly straight forward and quick to create.

Weapon icon switch animation
